`Tuples` are a collection of values of different types. They are immutable, which means that once declared, they cannot be changed.

To declare a `tuple`, we use brackets `( )` and separate the values with commas `,`:

x = (1, 2)

A lesser known fact is that you can declare a `tuple` without the brackets `( )` and it will still work:

x = 1, 2

this is called `tuple packing`. Similarly you can unpack a `tuple` into variables:

t = (1, 2) x, y = t print(x) # prints: 1 print(y) # prints: 2

You can even unpack a `tuple` from the values inline:

x, y = 1, 2 print(x) # prints: 1 print(y) # prints: 2

Though it is not often considered in terms of `Tuples`, this method is often used to assign multuple variables at once.
